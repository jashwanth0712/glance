# Posting Style Guide

**Established:** 2026-07-12

## Voice

- Keep posts crisp, natural, and human.
- Write conversationally in the first person, as a builder sharing real progress.
- Prefer simple language over polished corporate or promotional phrasing.
- Sound confident but not exaggerated; avoid buzzwords and hype.
- Use an occasional emoji when it feels natural, not as decoration.

## Structure

- Open with one short observation or question that feels familiar to builders.
- Keep paragraphs brief and easy to scan on mobile.
- Focus each post on one idea rather than explaining the whole product.
- Define Glance in one clear sentence.
- Mention the GrowthX Hermes Buildathon naturally near the end when relevant.
- Use only a small number of relevant hashtags.

## Product Language

Use this as the canonical short definition:

> Glance is an AI product compass that helps teams decide what to work on next, and what not to.

Supporting ideas:

- Building quickly is easier than ever, but speed does not guarantee direction.
- The important question is whether the team is building the right thing.
- Glance helps teams stay focused and ship what matters.

## Channel Notes

### Twitter/X

- Be especially concise and lead with the main thought immediately.
- Tag relevant accounts directly when mentioning the buildathon.
- Use short lines and minimal hashtags.

### LinkedIn

- Add enough context to make the problem relatable, but keep the post compact.
- Avoid long, overly polished launch narratives.
- End naturally rather than forcing an engagement question.

## Visual Direction

- Use a premium minimalist style with generous negative space.
- Prefer airy pastel blue gradients, soft diffusion, and clean centered typography.
- Keep the Glance title prominent and supporting copy concise.
- Reuse the same launch poster across Twitter/X and LinkedIn when appropriate.
- Avoid clutter, dashboards, stock imagery, loud neon colors, excessive effects, and watermarks.
